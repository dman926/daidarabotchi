I am an H1 tag. I am the biggest pure tag (no CSS), and I help with S.E.O. (search engine omptimization).

I am an H2 tag. Notice how we are on a new "line"? that's because structure tags (H1, p, div, etc.) are inheritely full-width.

I am an H6 tag. I am the smallest pure structural text tag.

I am a p tag. I am VERY useful for all the normal text and even for fun CSS tricks.

I am another p tag inside a div block (<div><p>Like this</p></div>). div tags are like p tags in structure, but they don't have any text styling, and are intended to hold other tags instead of text.


You can't see it, but there's a br tag above me. It's short for break, as in linebreak. This is a fancy tag called a self-closing tag. Self-closing tags are for tags that don't hold anything. They are writen like this: <br />

And above me is an hr tag. It's the same as a br tag, but it has a line divider in it.

I am a code tag. Because I am purely for style, I'm only as wide as I need to be, so I need to put a br tag after me to make the next tag on the next line.
I am an a tag. I am for links. Clicking me will take you to w3schools.com
